About Me

Hi! I am a PhD student advised by Prof. Shao-Kai Jian at Department of Physics and Engineering Physics, Tulane University. Previously, I studied as a master at School of Physics, Zhejiang University and was fortunately advised by Prof. Lih-King Lim since 2020. Before that, I studied as an undergraduate at School of Energy and Power, Guangdong University of Technology, where I worked with Prof. Kaige Hu in condensed matter theory from 2018 and Prof. Weitong Cai in catalytic chemistry from 2017. I am broadly interested in black holes, higher-dimensional holography, topological phases and critical states in quaiscrystals.

In recent years, I have focused on exploring topological states in quasicrystals [1], especially for:
(1) Topological phases in three-dimensional quasicrystals
(2) Fragile topological phases in quasicrystals
(3) Multifractal nature of topological surface states
